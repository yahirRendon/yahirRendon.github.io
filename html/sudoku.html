Sudoku is one of the world's most popular logic puzzles, but its modern form has a surprisingly international history. While early predecessors existed the first modern implementation is credited to an American architect, Howard Garns, in 1979 under the name Number Place. It later found its true home in Japan when the publisher Nikoli introduced it as Sudoku which is a shortened phrase meaning “the digits must remain single.” From there, it spread worldwide, becoming a staple of newspapers, puzzle books, and mobile games.
Today, Sudoku has a vibrant global community, complete with international championships and countless creative variants. Despite its many forms, the fundamental challenge remains the same: fill a 9 x 9 grid so that each row, column, and 3 x 3 box contains all digits from 1 to 9 exactly once. The result is an elegant blend of logic, pattern recognition, and satisfying deduction which keeps millions of players coming back for more.
